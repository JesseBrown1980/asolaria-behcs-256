The attached NovaLUM is not currently presented to Windows as a direct native NovaLUM USB endpoint. The physical path is mediated by another device and connector chain.

Current machine-side fact:

- Windows sees `USB Serial Port (COM9)`
- PnP ID: `USB\\VID_04D8&PID_000A\\5&237FFA2&0&3`

User-reported physical path:

- NovaLUM is connected through another device
- that other device is connected to power/charger
- another cable from that device connects to a nonstandard-looking connector on the back of the NovaLUM

Practical implication:

- raw direct USB assumptions are unsafe
- the machine may only be seeing a serial bridge or intermediary adapter
- local control should prefer:
  1. identifying the bridge role of `COM9`
  2. confirming whether the desktop sync app expects this serial bridge
  3. avoiding direct device-side configuration changes until the bridge path is understood
