After the NovaLUM was restarted and a human-style sync was triggered from the dashboard, the logs confirmed:

- the device settings remained in the restored Jesse/open state
- the NL2X app still detected the device on `COM9`
- the app read globals and firmware successfully
- the app still targeted `192.168.1.43:443`
- the local sync server accepted a real client connection

Evidence:
- `C:\Users\acer\Downloads\logs\NL2XSync_2026-03-29.log`
- `D:\projects\QDD\ebacmap-master\reports\novalum-sync-server-20260329-143516-enc.out.log`
- `D:\projects\QDD\ebacmap-master\reports\novalum-sync-server-20260329-143516-enc.err.log`

Critical sequence:
- app refreshed `COM9`
- device responded in binary mode
- globals read succeeded
- firmware read succeeded
- app reached `CanSync=True`
- sync-server logged `client connected ::ffff:192.168.1.43:58275`
- sync-server then logged `socket closed`
- server-side error was `read ECONNRESET`

Significance:
- the manual device settings are confirmed good enough for connection attempts
- the host IP and local listener are also confirmed good enough for connection attempts
- the remaining blocker is the earliest proxy/handshake phase after connect, not the dashboard, not COM9, and not the manual Wi-Fi configuration
