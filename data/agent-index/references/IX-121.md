# Human-like Systematic Generalization Through a Meta-Learning Neural Network
**Lake & Baroni, Nature 2023**

## Key Findings
- Standard neural networks fail at systematic generalization (combining known primitives in novel ways) — the long-standing challenge Fodor/Pylyshkin raised against connectionism
- MLC (Meta-Learning for Compositionality) solves this by training a neural network on a distribution of compositional learning tasks, not on a single task
- Achieves human-level systematic generalization on the SCAN benchmark and novel few-shot composition tasks
- The model learns to learn compositional rules — it extracts the algebraic structure from examples at test time
- Closes the gap between neural networks and symbolic systems without hardcoding symbolic rules

## How This Connects to Asolaria
- MLC is the theoretical ancestor of Asolaria's multi-stacking architecture — Jesse's BIML paper (IX-090) explicitly builds on MLC
- Asolaria agents need compositional generalization: combining skills, tools, and IX entries in novel combinations they were never explicitly trained on
- The meta-learning paradigm (learning to learn) maps directly to how Asolaria agents bootstrap new capabilities from existing IX chains

## Potential Application
MLC's few-shot compositional learning could be applied to Asolaria's skill correlator system, enabling agents to compose novel multi-tool workflows from a handful of demonstrated examples rather than explicit programming.
