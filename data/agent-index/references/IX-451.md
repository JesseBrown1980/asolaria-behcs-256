During the March 29 live sync attempts, the local encrypted sync-server accepted a client connection from `192.168.1.10:2000`.

This was confirmed as the NovaLUM device itself, not the desktop app path, because:
- ARP resolved `192.168.1.10` to MAC `80-c9-55-75-fb-90`
- that MAC exactly matches the device Wi-Fi MAC preserved in the globals readback

Evidence:
- `D:\projects\QDD\ebacmap-master\reports\novalum-sync-server-20260329-175500-enc.out.log`
- `C:\Users\acer\Downloads\logs\novalum-globals-after-write.json`
- ARP table on the laptop at the time of the sync attempt

Observed behavior:
- sync-server logged `client connected ::ffff:192.168.1.10:2000`
- the socket then ended without advancing further
- the desktop app log did not update during this attempt

Significance:
- the live device is now proven to be on the LAN and reaching the local sync-server directly over Wi-Fi
- this is a different path from the desktop app mediated `COM9` flow
- the active blocker is now the earliest direct device-to-server protocol phase after connect
