# Generative Agents: Interactive Simulacra of Human Behavior
**Park et al., Stanford/Google, 2023**

## Key Findings
- 25 generative agents inhabit a sandbox world (Smallville), forming relationships, planning, and collaborating with emergent social behaviors
- Architecture has three components: memory stream (raw observations), reflection (higher-level abstractions from memories), and planning (action sequences based on reflections)
- Agents retrieve memories by recency, importance, and relevance — a weighted scoring system, not simple FIFO
- Emergent behaviors arise without explicit programming: agents organize a Valentine's Day party, spread information through social networks, form new relationships
- The reflection mechanism is what separates meaningful agent behavior from simple prompt-completion — agents synthesize patterns from accumulated experience
