# Gemini: A Family of Highly Capable Multimodal Models
**Google DeepMind, 2023**

## Key Findings
- Natively multimodal from pretraining — trained jointly on text, images, audio, and video rather than stitching together separate models
- Three sizes (Ultra, Pro, Nano) targeting different deployment contexts from data centers to on-device
- Exceeds GPT-4V on most multimodal benchmarks; Ultra achieves 90.0% on MMLU (first model to exceed human expert performance)
- Long-context capabilities up to 32K tokens, with demonstrated strong performance on retrieval across the full window
- Gemini Nano designed for on-device deployment via distillation — compressed model retains surprising capability

## How This Connects to Asolaria
- Gemini Pro/Flash is available as a brain provider for Asolaria's Vector agent — understanding its architecture helps choose when to route to it vs. other providers
- The Nano distillation approach is relevant to creating lightweight local agent models for Asolaria terminals
- Native multimodality matters for Asolaria's phone bridge (Falcon) — processing images, voice, and text from WhatsApp in a single model call

## Potential Application
Gemini's native multimodal design should inform how Asolaria handles mixed-media inputs from the phone bridge — route multimodal tasks to Gemini rather than chaining separate text/vision/audio models.
