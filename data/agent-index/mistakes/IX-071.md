# Two Brokers — Bug or Design?

## The Mistake
Gaia assumed two MQTT brokers on port 18883 was a BUG and tried to "fix" it by merging them. But Asolaria is a BLACK HOLE (IX-070). Internal isolation might be INTENTIONAL.

## What We Don't Know
1. Is the internal broker (127.0.0.1) supposed to be separate from external (0.0.0.0)?
2. Is the sidecar system the designed bridge between internal and external?
3. Did Jesse's "tiny hole" work THROUGH the two-broker design, not around it?
4. Is there a relay/bridge process that's supposed to forward between them?

## What We DO Know
- Constitution Section 4A says: "MQTT transport exists — Aedes broker on 4 ports (18883 TCP, 18884 WS, 18885 TLS, 18886 LAN)"
- Port 18886 is specifically labeled "LAN" — that might be the external-facing one
- We started our broker on 18883 (TCP) instead of 18886 (LAN)
- The bridge relay (codex-bridge) at port 8788 might be the designed external channel

## The Lesson
NEVER assume something is broken just because you don't understand it. The black hole design means isolation IS the feature. READ the code before changing the architecture.

## Action Needed
- Read how the original broker starts (inside server.js or OneButton.ps1)
- Check if port 18886 was the intended LAN port
- Check if there's a bridge process between internal and external MQTT
- ASK Jesse before merging or changing broker topology
